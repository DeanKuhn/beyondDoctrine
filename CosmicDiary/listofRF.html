Common Rhetorical Fallacies

Additional ones added regularily.

Ad hominem is when one attacks the person making the argument rather than the argument itself.

Straw man is when one argues against an overly simplified or often misconstrued version of the opponentâ€™s viewpoint.

A red herring is when one brings up a completely different point when cornered in reasoning to divert attention away from a weaker issue and onto one they can make stronger.

Bandwagon is when one bases the validity of an argument on how many others (supposedly) do/believe the same thing (can also be used in the opposite way to stereotype people under a logically fallible claim).

Exaggeration is when one exaggerates claims, overstates arguments, or represents more than what is true.

Survivorship bias occurs when one focuses only on the successful outcomes of those who took certain actions or believed certain ways.
